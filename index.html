⚠ Key Seasonality Correction (v4.1): Calibrated from 44 observations including Jun 19 2026 LM_XB459/LM_XB456/LM_XB460 weekly reports. Confirmed pattern: Rib/Loin SP/CO ratios peak DEC (+7.4%), secondary JUN (+1%), trough MAR (-4.2%) (holiday entertaining, prime rib season, restaurant bookings) and trough in Jul when live cattle prices are also elevated, compressing the ratio. The absolute $/cwt price does peak Jun–Aug, but the ratio to cutout and live cattle is highest in fall. Chuck/Round remain flat. Brisket peaks Mar–May (BBQ season onset). This correction affects all ratio-based forecast outputs.

Ratio methodology: SP/CO = (Sub-Primal $/cwt) ÷ (Choice Cutout $/cwt). SP/LC = (Sub-Primal $/cwt) ÷ (5-area Fed Steer $/cwt). Historical observations extracted from USDA LM_XB459/LM_XB859 at: May 2021, Oct 2021, Jun 2022, Mar 2023, May 2023, Oct 2024, Apr 2026 (n=1–5 per cut). Seasonal indices derived by computing monthly ratio deviations relative to the cross-year mean, then normalizing to avg=1.000. Cuts with only 1 observation use the parent primal's seasonal template. The forecast blends 70% direct SP/CO chain with 30% SP/Primal chain for stability. SP/LC = SP/CO × (Cutout/LC ratio), so seasonal patterns in SP/LC reflect both the cut's own seasonal variation and the cutout/live ratio seasonality.
